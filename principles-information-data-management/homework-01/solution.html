Relationships Summary

RelationshipEntitiesCardinalityDescription
Manages (PI)Professor — Project1:N Each project has exactly one PI (Project = 1..1). A professor can manage multiple projects (Prof = 0..N).
Works_On (Co-I)Professor — ProjectM:N Each project has 1+ co-investigators (Project = 1..N). A professor can co-investigate multiple projects (Prof = 0..N).
SupervisesProfessor — Grad Student — ProjectTernary For each (GS, Project) pair, exactly one professor supervises. Every project has at least one GS RA (Project = 1..N). Note: The compound key constraint “(GS, Project) → Professor” cannot be expressed with single-entity arrow notation in the ternary; it is stated here as an additional constraint. Alternatively, this could be modeled using aggregation (enclosing GS–RA–Project in a dashed box, then connecting Supervises as a binary relationship to Professor).
Runs (Chairman)Professor — Department1:1 Each department has exactly one chairman (Dept = 1..1). A professor chairs at most one department (Prof = 0..1).
Works_InProfessor — DepartmentM:N Professors work in 1+ departments (Prof = 1..N). Relationship attribute: time_pct. (Dept = 0..N)
MajorGrad Student — DepartmentN:1 Each graduate student has exactly one major department (GS = 1..1, Dept = 0..N).
AdvisesGrad Student — Grad StudentN:1 (self) Each GS has one senior GS advisor (Advisee = 1..1). An advisor can advise many students (Advisor = 0..N).

Assumptions

  1. A professor can be chairman of at most one department.
  2. Not every professor necessarily manages a project (PI) or works on a project (co-I). These are separate relationships.
  3. A project's PI may or may not also be listed as a co-investigator; Manages and Works_On are independent.
  4. Not every graduate student is required to work on a project as a research assistant.
  5. Every graduate student has a senior graduate student advisor (as stated). The most senior GS may not have an advisor (logical boundary condition).

Problem 2: Prescriptions-R-X Pharmacy Database

Entities & Their Attributes

Pharmacynameaddress, phone_number

Relationships Summary

RelationshipEntitiesCardinalityDescription
Made_By (Identifying)Drug — Pharm. CompanyN:1 Each drug belongs to exactly one company (Drug = 1..1). Company can have many drugs (Company = 0..N). Weak entity identifying relationship.
Primary_DocPatient — DoctorN:1 Every patient has one primary physician (Patient = 1..1). Every doctor has at least one patient (Doctor = 1..N).
SellsPharmacy — DrugM:N Each pharmacy sells several drugs (Pharmacy = 1..N). A drug may not be sold everywhere (Drug = 0..N). Attribute: price.
PrescribesDoctor — Patient — DrugTernary (M:N:P) Doctors prescribe drugs for patients. Attributes: date, quantity. Key: (Doctor, Patient, Drug).
ContractPharm. Company — PharmacyM:N Long-term contracts. Attributes: start_date, end_date, text, supervisor.

Assumptions

  1. We assume name uniquely identifies each Pharmacy (the problem does not explicitly state "identified by name" for Pharmacy, unlike Patient/Doctor/Pharm. Company).
  2. A pharmaceutical company can exist in the database without currently having any drugs.
  3. Not every drug must be sold at a pharmacy (a drug can exist in the database without being stocked).
  4. The supervisor for each contract is modeled as an attribute of the Contract relationship, since no Supervisor entity or attributes are defined in the requirements.

Constraints NOT Captured by the ER Diagram

  1. Temporal prescription constraint: "If a doctor prescribes the same drug for the same patient more than once, only the last such prescription needs to be stored." The ER model stores current state and does not capture this update/replacement constraint.
  2. Supervisor change over time: "The contract supervisor can change over the lifetime of the contract." The ER diagram only captures the current supervisor, not the history of supervisor changes.
  3. Supervisor NOT NULL: "There must always be a supervisor for each contract." NOT NULL constraints on relationship attributes are not explicitly expressed in standard ER notation.
  4. Pharmacy as supervisor source: "Pharmacies appoint a supervisor for each contract" — the constraint that the supervisor must be associated with the pharmacy is not captured.
  5. Drug deletion cascade: "If a pharmaceutical company is deleted, you need not keep track of its products any longer." While the weak entity model implies existential dependence, the specific cascade delete behavior is an operational constraint enforced at the DBMS level.

ER Diagram Notation Legend

Rectangle = Entity   |   Double Rectangle = Weak Entity

Diamond = Relationship   |   Double Diamond = Identifying Relationship

Oval = Attribute   |   Underlined = Primary Key   |   Dashed Underline = Partial Key (Discriminator)

Thick line = Total participation (at least one)   |   Thin line = Partial participation (can be zero)

Arrow (from entity toward diamond) = Entity participates at most once   |   No arrow = Many

Combination: Thin + No arrow = 0..N  |  Thick + No arrow = 1..N  |  Thin + Arrow = 0..1  |  Thick + Arrow = 1..1
